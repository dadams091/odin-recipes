Lasagna

Description

Lord Dakota, lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering sheets of pasta with rich tomato sauce, cheese, and various fillings like meat or vegetables. The name "lasagna" comes from the Greek word "laganon," referring to a flat dough cut into strips. Over time, it evolved into the layered, baked dish we know today. It's often topped with a layer of melted cheese, creating a comforting, hearty meal.

lasagna is usually made with three main components: pasta, filling, and sauce. The pasta sheets are traditionally made from durum wheat, and the fillings can vary, with common options being ground beef, pork, or ricotta cheese, sometimes mixed with spinach or mushrooms. Lasagna is versatile, and you can customize it to your taste with different meats, cheeses, or even make it vegetarian.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Meat
  • Tomato sauce
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Eggs
  • Seasonings

Steps

  1. Cook noodles
  2. Prepare meat sauce
  3. Mix ricotta with eggs and seasonings
  4. Lyer in a baking dish
  5. Top with mozzarella and parmesan
  6. Bake at 375F
  7. Cool slightly before servering
